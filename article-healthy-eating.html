Healthy Eating on a Busy Schedule

Maintaining a healthy diet doesn't have to be a full-time job. Even with a packed schedule, small changes can lead to significant improvements in your well-being.

1. The Power of Meal Prepping

Spend just two hours on Sunday preparing basics: wash and cut vegetables, cook a batch of grains, or boil eggs. Having ready-to-use ingredients reduces the temptation to order fast food when you're tired.

2. Keep it Simple

Healthy meals don't need 20 ingredients. A piece of protein, a large portion of greens, and some healthy fats (like avocado or olive oil) is a complete meal that takes 15 minutes to assemble.

3. Smart Snacking

Avoid the "vending machine trap." Keep nuts, fruits, or Greek yogurt in your bag or at your desk. High-protein snacks keep you full longer and prevent energy crashes.

4. Hydration is Key

Often, we mistake thirst for hunger. Drink water throughout the day. It improves concentration, aids digestion, and keeps your metabolism active.

Remember, consistency is better than perfection. Use tools like FridgeChef to find quick ideas for the healthy items you already have!
